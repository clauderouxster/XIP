1. Guidelines :

References to the organisation in general (a structure consisting of organisation members that do speak with a collective voice and has a charter, statute or defined aims).

- association between the org and its members, other orgs, products, services :

* description of the organisation of the company : the org employs, pers of org, org executives, delegates of org. (= PREP(OF), SUBJ of certain verbs, MOD_entitregie)
* description of seats : the org was founded, the headquarters of org are (= SUBJ of certain verbs,PREP(OF))
* rel org-its product/services : org workstations, org shares, org's hardware (= MOD_ENTITREGIE, POSSES)
* rel org-org or org-GPE : org and org alliance, org teams with, org joins org, org team (= MOD_entitregie,SUBJ of certain verbs)

- org as an amalgam of all its components
rare

- org as target of actions or attitudes :

* target of economic or political actions that are directed at the org as a whole :
buy/sue org,
not boycott = product

* emotional attitudes towards an org and judgements of an org as a whole:
to hate org, org is great company (= OBJ of certains verbs, ATTRIB)

- commercial actions (org-org,org-product)
product developed by org, offerings from org, org provides (= SUBJ of certains verbs,PREP(FROM)
sell, buy, spend, invest

B/ ORGANISATION-FOR-MEMBERS
nb occurrences : 220 / 1090 (20.2%)
**************************

1. Guidelines :

"People who work for or represent the organisation as well as suborganisations of the org (members states of an international org)."

a) Instigators of communicative actions:

speak, announce, say, declare, discuss, advertise (a_communic,a_soc_inter)
sign, write, sue, bid, fight, agree (legal communications/actions)

b) the instigators of decisions, attitudes and plans/goals:

decide, want, think, have to
"IBM's own development goals, Nestle had to..."

c) the experiencers of emotional or mental states

emotional states : fear, panic, joy, anxiety (expert_subj,EXPE)

d) the recipient of actions that are not political or economic actions directed at the org as a whole and that can only be directed at people (football team, justice) (RCP)


Subtype depending of the kind of members : off, pop, others

e) Possible confusions :
- stock phrases
- association bewteen attributes/artifacts and an org : 
	if official or global (flag, charters, pension system,language, uniforms) = literal
	if attributes ascribed to the org as a unique legal entity (Microsoft is powerful) = literal
	if the attribute is associated with people and are non official = meto
	if the artifact belongs ti or come from the whole org = literal (IBM workstations, Red Cross van)
- economic situations, attributes or actions = literal
	org's income, expenditure (IBM has debts, Toyota's financial profit, IBM is rich)
- economic actions that can be targeted at the place as a whole = literal
	buy IBM

1. Guidelines :

tout ce qui correspond à "product" ou "artifact" ds WN.
"artifacts supposed to be sold in shops or exported"
examples:
IBM users 
boycott Nestle
the largest Fiat

2. Comment les reconnaitre (pour détail voir ci-dessous)

 2.1 indices forts : 

a) détermination : pronom possessif > det indéfini (mais rare) > det défini (attention, possibilité de confusion avec litéral)

gestion des determinants :
	-  enlever advbs DETD(mot, ADV:...) (erreurs la plupart du temps)
	-  possessifs : ne pas prendre en compte le 3P (it): a enlever avec le lemme, on garde tous les autres. Sinon le 		deter possessif a l'air tres discriminant pour les products, même s'il se rencontre tout seul, on peut dire que l'EN 		est une org-for-product.
	-  dets indéfinis : normalement pas de pb.
	-  dets définis : discriminant si associés avec marque du pluriel et adjectif mais attention, il 			existe des cas ou c'est du literal
	Dans quels cas ne pas considérer le det comme indice de product :
		si det a une majuscule
		si det + orghead/orgend dans l'EN alors literal
		si det + POSSES(workstation,board,store,supermarket,superstore,skill,monopoly,vehicle,recorder) alors literal.rq : pas bcp de POSSES pour les products (aucun ds corpus d'entrainement).
		si det tout seul alors literal
		si det + EN en SUBJ d'un verbe a forte connotation literal alors literal (ca sera fait par defaut)
		si det + nom de NADJ de country avant alors literal (souvent ds un MOD)
		si det + PREP_of(part,staff, subsidiary,board,director general,symbol,president) alors literal
		si det + PREP
	---> hormis tous ces cas, on peut dire que c'est product.

b) marque du pluriel : attention xip ne gère pas toujours

 2.2 autres indices :

rôle grammatical : certaines PREP > QUALIF(org,adj) ou MOD_METORECT(org,adj) > OBJ-N(vb,org-prod) > SUBJ-N(vb,org-prod) > MOD_METOREG(mot,org)


travail lexiques :

pour les verbes en obj et subj
pour les modifieurs

a) les bonnes PREP :

PREP_OF(price/door/glass,org-prod)
PREP_AT(look,org-prod)
PREP_BETWEEN(mot,org-prod) --->lexique : si mot=collision --->product, si mot=agreement,etc.--->literal
PREP_WITH(mot,org-prod) attention, confusion avec members, prendre avec : problem 
PREP_IN(mot,org-prod)

les bonnes combinaisons : (pl optionnel)

- OBJ +DET +PL +QUALIF ou MODadj

- SUBJ + DET +QUALIF ou MODadj

- si que MODREG, c'est mieux si +DET +PL (---> a retrouver pour le mot recteur, e.g, "IBM users")

- si que DETdefini, c'est mieux si +PL

- POSSES(org,mot) : il faut que mot=artifact (WN?) et c'est mieux si DET (sur mot)
