Hervé J. Gallaire

Hervé J. Gallaire is president of the Xerox Innovation Group and Xerox's chief technology officer. He was appointed to this position in October 2001 and was named a corporate senior vice president in December 1999.

Gallaire is responsible for maximizing the company's billion-dollar annual investment in research and development. He is in charge of worldwide research and technology organizations in the U.S., Canada and Europe; intellectual property management and licensing; the engineering center; and several technology companies in the Xerox portfolio.

As head of research and technology, Gallaire leads an organization that is one of the world's most prolific generators of patentable ideas, with world-renowned research ongoing at the Xerox Research Centre Europe, the Xerox Research Centre of Canada, the Wilson Center for Research and Technology, Imaging and Services Technology Center and PARC Inc.

He joined Xerox in 1992 and managed the Xerox Research Centre Europe in Grenoble, France, from 1993 to 1998. He has served as chief architect of the corporation and vice president of the Xerox Architecture Center, and most recently, senior vice president for Xerox Research and Technology.

Before joining Xerox, Gallaire headed the department of mathematics and computer science at l'École Nationale Supérieure de l'Aéronautique et de l'Espace in France.

Gallaire holds a master of science degree and a doctorate in electrical engineering and computer science from the University of California, Berkeley, and a master's degree in mechanical engineering from École Nationale Supérieure des Arts et Métiers in France. He received the first distinguished alumnus award from Berkeley's EECS department and was elected a founding member of France's Académie des technologies.
